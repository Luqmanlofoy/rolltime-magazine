By Luqman Lofoy | Published: July 25, 2025

The skateboarding world has undergone significant changes since the pandemic began in 2020. What started as a period of isolation and closed skate parks turned into a renaissance for street skating and DIY spots.

With traditional skate parks closed, skaters returned to the streets, rediscovering the raw essence of skateboarding. This shift has led to a new appreciation for urban landscapes and unconventional spots.

One of the most notable developments has been the explosion of DIY skate spots. From backyard ramps to unauthorized concrete pours in abandoned lots, skaters have taken matters into their own hands.

Local skaters transformed an empty lot into a thriving skate spot (Video: ROLLTIME Magazine)

This DIY ethic has brought communities together in new ways. Skaters who might have only crossed paths at skate parks are now collaborating on projects and sharing resources.

As we move forward, it's clear that skateboarding has been permanently changed by this period. The combination of increased digital connectivity through social media and the physical return to street skating has created a unique moment in skate culture.
